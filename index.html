How to Sell Your Products to Indonesian Market
for Dummies

Frequently Asked Questions (Q&A)

What is BPOM, and why is it necessary for product registration?
BPOM (Badan Pengawas Obat dan Makanan) is the Indonesian FDA, responsible for ensuring the safety and quality of food, beverages, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals before they can be sold in the Indonesian market.

What is BPJPH, and why is it important for halal certification?
BPJPH (Badan Penyelenggara Jaminan Produk Halal) ensures that products comply with halal standards. All imported halal products must obtain certification through BPJPH or an authorized foreign halal body with an MRA (Mutual Recognition Agreement).

Do I need a halal certificate for all products?
Yes, if the product is categorized as halal. Halal certification is not required for certain non-halal products, such as alcohol or pork-based products.

What is a Prior Notice, and why is it required?
Prior Notice is a shipment registration process via notice.karantinaindonesia.go.id to comply with Indonesia’s quarantine and import requirements. It must be completed before the shipment arrives.

What are the special requirements for importing dairy products?
The factory must be registered with SIMREK Indonesia (Veterinary Public Health).

Is BPOM registration required for dairy products?
Yes, BPOM registration is mandatory for all imported dairy products.

What documents are needed to import frozen fruits and vegetables?
A Phytosanitary Certificate is required for registration and licensing.

Can I sell organic products without certification?
No, an organic certificate issued by a recognized certification body is required.

What are the common reasons for BPOM registration rejection?
- Incomplete documentation (missing lab test results, packaging details, etc.)
- Non-compliance with Indonesian food safety regulations
- Improper labeling (e.g., missing Indonesian translation)
